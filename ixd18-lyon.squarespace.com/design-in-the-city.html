Design in the city

Then & now…

Lyon has always been a city of social and economic opportunities. The city brings together a unique sense of business and a grand history of arts and culture. From the printing masters at the early days of the publishing industry, to the worldwide reputation of the silk workers during the Renaissance, the city never ceased to welcome technological breakthroughs. This is also in Lyon where the film-making industry found its roots, thanks to Auguste and Louis Lumière with their modern version of the “cinématographe”. The spirit of those entrepreneurs and craftsmen appears in all the well-established creative industries including video games, robotics, bio-technologies.

 

Videogames

From the early 80’s, Lyon has been at the very heart of the videogame industry. From tiny studios to leading game publishers, she is still welcoming the “crème de la crème”. Electronic Arts, Atari, Arkane Studio, Ivory Tower, Namco Bandai; these are just some of the names that drive the field forward and that legitimate the graduation programs available in Lyon’s schools.

Lyon French Tech

The official flagship for innovation and entrepreneurship, Lyon French Tech is a non profit organisation which acts as an entry point for people with innovative ideas. They help provide the resources necessary to accelerate the development of their projects. Lyon French Tech is the second largest digital ecosystem in France after Paris with 7,000 companies representing 42,000 jobs.

 

Robotics

A dense industrial base and a high concentration of digital companies make Lyon the ideal soil for the development of robotics and internet of things. This is a key sector in the economic strategy of Grand Lyon, which aims to become a reference area for everything related to robotics. In 2015, the Coboteam initiative was launched, to unite and lead the ecosystem and the emergence of a regional robotics industry.

 

City of the Future

As the number one Smart City in France in 2013 (M2OCity), Lyon has put the concept of merging economic growth and sustainability at the heart of its strategy. Four large urban projects (Confluence, Part-Dieu, Gerland & Carré de Soie) lead the way as living labs towards imagining and building new visions for the city of tomorrow. Four themes have emerged: new mobility, digital services, smart energy-grids and fostering innovation.

 

Now let’s talk about design!

 

Lyon is populated with a dense network of design people gathered in groups and non-profit associations, around lively coworking spaces and freelance-friendly cafés scattered among the city. IxDA is one of them and is stirring a great number of people around its monthly gatherings. We also can count on the Lyon branch of FLUPA, but also on vibrant places and spaces dedicated to design at its broader sense: coworking spaces like l’Atelier des Médias and La Cordée, incubators and creative spaces like Pôle Pixel or Youfactory (collaborative workshop for objects and product prototyping).

Lyon is the host of one of the biggest French web conference: Blend Web Mix, which reached a peak of nearly 1800 attendants in 2015. Lyon also welcomed WWW2012 and is now confirmed as host of the 2018 edition too, being the first city ever since 24 years to be host for the second time.
