The beautiful city of Lyon

Without any doubt, Lyon is the most beautiful city in France

— obviously no bias here! —

 

Lyon is the perfect choice, just to roam the banks of the Rhône and sip a delicious local beer while watching the sunset near the river, or wander in a city gifted with an amazing historical background. From the medieval Vieux-Lyon to the brand new and energy-savvy Confluence district (that you can reach on a vintage vaporetto boat along the Saône river), Lyon has a lot to show and give.

 

In Lyon, anyone can have a taste of France in what it does best.

 

This perfectly-sized town offers the widest panel of food, architecture and bits of the French culture. With a wide range of public transports (metro, tram, tentacular network of rental bikes…) and as many restaurants as you can dream of, Lyon will keep you busy and amazed during your free time. You can walk its famous center, the Presqu’île, in about an hour or so, and go from the top of the Croix-Rousse hill and its typical country town atmosphere, through the wide streets of the Presqu’île packed with nice boutiques, to the Confluences Museum and its magnificent architecture. Its summers are just about warm, its winters are not even rude and enlightened by the now worldwide renowned grandiose light celebrations on December 8th.

 

Food culture

 

Hometown of Paul Bocuse and many famous chefs, Lyon will drive you mad with its hundreds of restaurants. Flagship dishes include the “tablier de sapeur” (literally meaning sapper’s apron – a dish of pan-fried tripe), the “quenelle” (a mixture of creamed fish, chicken, or meat in wheat semolina with a light egg binding), the “salade lyonnaise” ( lettuce with bacon, croûtons and a poached egg) and many more. Of course, the main dishes are always followed by cheese AND desert! Don’t plan on following any diet here, but the one of good, rich, fine food!

 
 

Lifestyle

There’s a lot to do in Lyon all year round. In May, the “Nuits sonores” festival preps attendees for a warm summer. In June and July, the antique theater is turned into the most gorgeous venue to welcome dozens of famous musicians for the Nuits de Fourvière. There is a rich night culture around Lyon’s most active districts: the very center is packed with bars and nightclubs.

The Rhône and the Saône offer their banks to passersby to relax during endless evenings where they can have a drink on the many péniches, typical river boats turned into bars and music venues.

 
 

Fête des Lumières

Today, Lyon is renowned worldwide for its “Fête des Lumières” (Festival of Lights), a yearly event taking place in early December, with its pivotal day on the 8th of this very month. This uniquely Lyonnaise tradition pays tribute to Virgin Mary who “saved” the city from plague in 1643 and dictates that every house place candles along the outsides of windows. Today, the festival showcases installations and pieces from numerous modern artists throughout all districts of the city. It lasts for several days in a row and attracts 4 million visitors roaming through the enlightened streets.
