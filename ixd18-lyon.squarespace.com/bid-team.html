Bid team

Availability

Making room for this bid means making time for the event to come. Part of the team is working at 80%, and some of us are willing to take more time to properly organize the Interaction Week.

Motivation

Want some data to support that? We've spent around 400 hours preparing this bid, including more than 200 emails, and a few hours of Skype calls. Still counting the Basecamp to-dos…

Diversity

We are coming from different places, with different backgrounds. But we share two things together: love for the city of Lyon and the energy to help the design community grow.

International

Our team is composed of people from all around Europe, who either love Lyon, are from Lyon or are currently living in Lyon. Most of us spend time around the world: Switzerland, New Zealand, Rwanda…

Art + design

We are arts enthusiasts. Besides our roles in the design community, we are photographers, music listeners and practitioners, sketchers, museum wanderers, dancers, etc.

Perfectionists

Even though we know how to get things done, we aim at the very best in what we do.

Meet the team!

From top to bottom and from left to right: Fleur, Marie-Cécile, Caroline, Claudia, Jean-Yves, Jean-Baptiste, Guillaume & Bertrand.

 

Fleur (LinkedIn & Twitter)
Fleur is a design activist. She has a natural taste for giving a hand in design projects, and this motivation led her to the Cité du Design in Saint-Étienne, France. Over there, she was in charge of supporting companies to solve design issues in the Auvergne & Rhône-Alpes area. Now she leads a design management curriculum, a project she co-created with a renowned Dutch company and which starts to resonate within the French companies’ world. Fleur joined the IxDA team in 2015 and volunteered 2 years for UX Lausanne. 

Marie-Cécile (LinkedIn & Twitter)
The best Lyon ambassador here in our team! Marie-Cécile fell in love with Lyon when she moved there to pursue her applied arts studies, and she works and lives there since 1999. Be it for promoting design or her dear city, she is as passionate and dedicated. Marie-Cécile is an active member of the French design community in Lyon, but also Paris and Bordeaux, where she likes to give talks here and there. She is also a writer about design practices and fluent in English, which is of interest regarding our international attendees.

Caroline (LinkedIn & Twitter)
Caroline is a freelance Art Director focused on web and natural interfaces. In everyday life, her taste for research and knowledge acquisition helps her lead her company. She is driven by a huge generosity which motivated her to participate in the IxDA meetings and to join the founding team in 2011. 

Claudia (LinkedIn & Twitter)
Claudia already has a serious resume when it comes to conferences. As a participant first, and now as a volunteer (for UX Lausanne for instance). Claudia’s “real” work is design in the broadest sense. From Munich and Leipzig, her various experiences gave her a wide view of information design as well as software edition. Today, she also focuses on graphic facilitation and co-conception. Let’s bet that Interaction Week 2018 in Lyon will be her final challenge to speak french like a local!

Jean-Yves (LinkedIn & Twitter)
Jean-Yves is IxDA Lyon’s president and founding member. His implication and energy to share design practices are two key ingredients that make IxDA Lyon the successful organization that it is. His strong experience in project management also keeps the team together. Jean-Yves is also a designer in a big company in Lyon, which makes him travel throughout France. Maybe one of the reasons why his network is so dense… Or maybe it can be explained by his genuine and contagious happiness!

Jean-Baptiste (LinkedIn & Twitter)
Jean-Baptiste (aka JB) is a ‘mind opener.’ His curiosity and will to share his discoveries lead him to Villefontaine, where he coordinates the interaction design master degree. Fully invested on his students’ side, he coaches them on diverse projects with companies, living labs and organizations in the area; together, they reinvent the city, experiment new services and imagine our digital future. Naturally Jean-Baptiste joined IxDA in its early days. He is still inspiring the local team with hot topics, is bringing in new people and is creating the link between disciplines. His transversal approach and his complete commitment to design education makes him a great asset to our bid team and for the Education Summit!

Guillaume (LinkedIn & Twitter)
Guillaume is the Art Director of Antistatique in Lausanne. Beforehand, he graduated in Lyon and built a large part of his design career over there. He has been an active IxDA member since 2011 and spoke at Interaction 14 in Amsterdam and this year in Helsinki. He is also vice president of the IxDA Lyon chapter.

Bertrand (LinkedIn & Twitter)
Bertrand is our Zen master, advising our team with soft and benevolent strength. His position as a consultant and director of the Wax Agency in Lyon doesn’t allow him to dedicate as much time as he would like to, but he knows when to make himself available to push things forward. Bertrand is also in charge of the FLUPA Lyon chapter, as part of the French UXPA. As a great leader, he often gives talks in conferences in Paris and Lyon.
