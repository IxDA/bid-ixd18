They support us

 

Therefore we reached out to many local organizations and experienced great support. We are confident to be able to build this conference with IxDA Lyon in the lead, and with a strong network of partners to actively support us.

We presented our vision of Interaction Week 2018 in Lyon to people around us: it turns out they are thrilled!

Ed Hazlewood, Lyon Convention Bureau

Supporters from Lyon

LIRIS research lab investigates images in every sense, particularly information systems using or creating images. Currently their researchers focus on data recording and visualization, which raises many questions on ethics and the use of this data. Pretty hot topics we are impatient to debate by 2018!

—

“One of our research teams is actively pursuing research at the intersection of Interaction Design and Human Computer Interaction. (…) We will be particularly engaged in the Education summit (…).”

Liris's letter

Erasme is a living lab exploring digital solutions to serve the community: smart city, knowledge transfer, museography or social actions in general are their main interests. Ideas emerge in this lab that gathers designers, engineers, researchers, social workers and user communities; outcomes are tested and implemented in the city of Lyon. A great asset for our city that you will discover along your visit.

—

“We would love to host creative events in our lab or with our open community. As co-founders of Museomix, we think great opportunities arise from the encounter of interaction designers and other innovators.”

Erasme's letter

École Normale Supérieure de Lyon is considered as one of the three most prestigious and best French universities. It offers a master program in Information Architecture since 2011.

—

“Our master in Information Architecture teaches among other topics Interaction Design, UX and UI design, and our students would greatly enjoy and benefit from participating to Interaction week.”

ENS's letter
 

La French Tech is a brand recently created to promote French entrepreneurship initiatives. Behind this label, there are many actions: innovation projects support, bursaries for startupers, communication on projects in France but also internationally. The Lyon French Tech chapter is particularly focused on user centered innovation and digital products. It make total sense they join Interaction Week 2018 to pursue their mission!

Lyon French Tech's letter

Imaginove is one of the most active and creative clusters in the area! It gathers companies, labs and startups from different backgrounds around transmedia, IoT, AI or video games.

As interfaces are moving out of screens, their scope of interests is just blowing up! As confirmed event coordinators, we can count on this team to find the right people to setup kick-ass workshops, hackathons or maker events!

Imaginove's letter

Tubà is an experimental place and a place for experimentation in the heart of Lyon. The team there has the mission to gather startups, large companies and institutions to reinvent the city. They host workshops on different urban topics, offer budding projects an open place to be tested, and organize plenty of great events to encourage entrepreneurship. A crossroad in the economical heart of the city that could be used during Interaction Week 2018 and – for sure – a unique place to visit!

Tubà's letter
 

La Cuisine du Web was born in 2012, acknowledging that the Rhône-Alpes area had resources and spirit close to what we can find in the Silicon Valley, but needed an impulse. Since then, it has been focusing on building a Web ecosystem, enabling entrepreneurs, start ups, freelances and agencies to rally and create an interoperable network. Inspired by the success of Lyonnais cuisine, this organization defines itself as an “action-tank.” Its flagship event is the Blend Web Mix conference.

I.D.E.A. is a unique program created by Centrale and EM Lyon business school to push Design Thinking methods in innovation projects. I.D.E.A. students launched most of the digital startups in the area and are great ambassadors of UX design methods. Having them on board is a great opportunity to discuss design management in a startup context and also for larger companies that are trying to integrate startup and design methods.

Intervening on behalf of a large range of public and private customers in France and worldwide, GL Events's mission is assisting companies, institutions and event organisers at every stage of the process from the definition of their event strategies to final implementation in the field. Working alongside local and regional government, it contributes to policies promoting the attractiveness of territories, by adopting a dynamic and an ambitious approach to managing the venues under its management.
