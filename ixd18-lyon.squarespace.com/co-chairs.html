Ciao, I’m Roberta

Roberta Tassi – fellow at Frog

Hi, I’m Roberta. Globetrotter, eclectic designer and tireless researcher all in one.

From the launch of servicedesigntools.org back in 2009, I have been working at the intersection of interaction and service design, with a specific focus on combining design ethnography, participatory design and information visualization to solve complex systemic challenges. 

Frog has been my playground since 2010. I have been a Design Research lead, worked with post-it notes across all continents, founded a temporary office in Nairobi, brought some beautiful service design projects to life and – most of all – enjoyed stretching my skills on a variety of innovation programs and learning new things, every single day.

I love teaching UCD (professor at Politecnico of Milan), working on social impact initiatives (fellow at UX for Good), facilitating discussions (organizer at WIAD 2014, Milan) and contributing to the global dialogue (upcoming conference: EPIC 2016).

My passion for research and knowledge sharing has brought me here, proposing myself as co-chair to facilitate the conversations that will redefine our view on interaction design moving forward, with a special attention on how we apply technology to design life-changing solutions for both developed and emerging countries.

Bonjour, I’m Gilles

Gilles Demarty – UX Architect at :ratio

Hi, I’m Gilles. Chances are you already know me, but let me list how I can contribute to Interaction Week 2018 and why I do believe Interaction Week should be in Lyon.

Part of my duty at the studio :ratio is to organize UX Lausanne, a 2-days conference that we’ve been organizing for 3 years around the subject of User Experience. I have also participated in the organization of Interaction14’s program, have been giving a few talks, mostly in French, and last year, I started to teach engineers about User Centered Design in Lyon.

I am deeply attached to IxDA and always tried to contribute to it in a humble way: first by creating a chapter in Lyon, then a second in Switzerland. Later on, I helped Birgit develop the core team. Now as a regional coordinator I support chapters all over Europe in their development.

I want to bring Interaction Week to Lyon, because I believe the city resonates in meaningful ways with IxDA: As a melting pot of cultures, highly rooted in craftsmanship, rebellious by nature. Bringing Interaction Week here will be the trigger to a truly deep transformation of the city’s design community.
