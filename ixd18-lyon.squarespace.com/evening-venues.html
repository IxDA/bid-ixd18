Evening venues

 
 

Musée des Confluences

 

This distinctive and grandiose building will stun you, wether you like its peculiar architecture or not. Our brand new Museum dedicated to anthropology and science leaves nobody indifferent. It is located at the very bottom of the Presqu’île, where the rivers Rhône and Saône meet and merge. This “floating crystal cloud of stainless steel and glass” immediately attracts the eyes of the bystanders and greets all travelers coming from the South. Its three permanent collections aim at answering the many questions of our world’s origins, as well as ours. Both outside and inside, the Musée des Confluences was born from a tremendous design work, from its gigantic structure up to the tiniest details of the typographic work done on the labels of its many masterpieces.

The Musée des Confluences can offer cocktail spaces for 50 to 600 attendees. According to us, it would make a great opening party place.

 
Featured
 

Le Sucre

 

Is there a better place to party than a top of a former industrial building turned into a place of modern art exhibitions and events? Not only Le Sucre is part of the hip places in here, but it offers a gorgeous view over the Rhône and the Saône.

Le Sucre offers a space for 300 to 800 party attendees.
We’d see it as perfect for a closing party!

 
 

Les Subsistances

 

Another great place of art, Les Subsistances are described as an international laboratory for artistic creation. The buildings are classified as “monument historique” and formed of an old monastery and industrial buildings. Its massive inner courtyard, La Verrière, can welcome up to 2000 people. Part of Les Subsistances also welcomes the École Nationale des Beaux Arts.

Les Subsistances can welcome up to 2000 people for a standing cocktail.

 
Featured
 

Le Transbordeur

 

This famous concert venue can be turned into a wicked party space, when it doesn’t welcome international artists setting fire to an ever growing crowd. Le Transbordeur is famous for its perfect size and hip atmosphere.

Le Transbordeur can welcome 600 people in its club, and much more in the main room (1,500 people).

 
 

Ninkasi Gerland

 

In Lyon, Ninkasi is a local institution: this franchise of restaurants is famous for its delicious and cheap homebrewed beers. The Ninkasi Gerland is the biggest among them. This venue is famous for hosting many open stage gigs, salsa evenings and many other events. Ninkasi’s burgers are also quite renowned, another good reason to appreciate this lively and colorful place.

The Ninkasi Gerland can feed and please up to 1,500 attendees.

 
 

Brasserie Georges

 

Another local institution in the city of Lyon, the very famous restaurant Brasserie Georges stands undefeated: it is the oldest brasserie of the city and one of the oldest in Europe. Its delightful Art Nouveau decoration and stunning architecture unveil a tour de force: 710 m² of ceiling are only supported by four 25-foot fir beams, which leaves a massive dining room totally free of pillars or structure.

Brasserie Georges offers a private 100-seats room which would make a great staff/speakers dinner.
