Venues

 

Not only does Lyon have a great architectural and historical background, but it also is a city in movement, with a large venues offer as surprising as boats on the river Rhône or a glass room atop the Opéra. Among the many ancient and contemporary venues it bears, we made a selection of the ones which we think might be just perfect in their size and atmosphere to welcome the attendees as well as the speakers.

 

Our first option
The historic city center

 

We chose to use an awe-inspiring space to enjoy the conference, while still connecting with the historical side of Lyon. What is better than an opera venue to convey the right energy for the conference?

The Opéra de Lyon welcomes 1,300 attendees in its main room, and 250 participants in breakup rooms. It also provides alternative spaces for 200 people for cocktails, lunches and dinners.

The Opéra in the details

This gorgeous location is one of the most noticeable buildings of the city. It is the one that triggered our interest to welcome Interaction Week 2018. Right in the center of the historical district of the Presqu’île and first opened in 1831, the building has known a quite uncanny revamping with the intervention of the distinguished French architect Jean Nouvel in 1985. Jean Nouvel tripled the space available, not only below ground to create rehearsal space, but also most strikingly above the original building, where he created a steel and glass barrel vault. This achievement was called “an architectural tour de force” where past meets future in a perfect and respectful combination. At night, the roof lighting reacts to the audience’s cheering…

The main room can host 1,300 attendees.

 
 

Salle Rameau

The Salle Rameau is a theater, usually welcoming street comedians more than interaction designers. Its “art-nouveau” style offers a very unique experience to its the attendees. This great location, 7 minutes walk away from the Opéra, gives a bit of time to reset our attendees’ mind. 

The Salle Rameau easily welcomes 469 attendees. 

Chapelle de la Trinité

The chapel from the 17th century is Lyon’s jewel of baroque architecture that can be arranged from a conference setting to a dinner setting or even a catwalk. Due to its outstanding acoustics it has been hosting concerts ever since its consecration. It is located in the heart of Presqu’île, just a 5 minute walk away from the Opéra.

The chapel welcomes 500 attendees in its main room while still including a small catering in the front.

Musée des Beaux Arts

The Museum of Fine Arts offers delightful scenery for smaller events and breaks. It is one of the largest art museums in France and is set up in a former Benedictine convent of the 17th and 18th centuries, offering a gorgeous inner courtyard with arcades and stunning rooms like the Réfectoire Baroque. It is very close to the Opéra, just a 2 minutes walk.

The Musée des Beaux Arts offers spaces at various capacity: the Garden can welcome 500 attendees, the Réfectoire 250 and the Auditorium 180.

Our second option
The alternative industrial area

 

For our second choice, we have yet another unique location, from the industrial era. Its location in the Confluence Quarter makes it completely in line with our vision.

Where Lyon’s 2 rivers meet, the new Confluence area is emerging from a former industrial district. This massive project of urban renewal combines bold architecture, restored industrial sites and re-embraces its river banks and natural environment. A place where creativity and entrepreneurship are all around.

La Sucrière in the details

La Sucrière is a famous building devoted to contemporary art, situated on the banks of the Saône in the hip Confluence Quarter. This former factory warehouse was used to store sugar, as its name may reveal. Abandoned for 3 decades, it was completely revamped in 2003 by William Vassal and is now a place of exhibitions, one of the most famous being the Biennale of Contemporary Art in Lyon since 2007. This venue is huge and very distinctive. Its facade is regularly painted anew and left to the creativity of many artists. It is topped by Le Sucre, a rooftop cocktail bar hosting many trendy events.

La Sucrière can be configured in many ways: The main room hosts 1,000 persons and there are 2 break-out rooms of 300 each. The mezzanine allows for a 1,000m² of space for sponsors and catering. 

 
 

2 more options…

Les Subsistances

We see Les Subsitances as an ideal evening venue, but it could also be considered as a conference venue. The location offers a large covered courtyard that could be configured as the main conference room, plus there is 3 possible breakout rooms available.

1,000 people for the main room, with 3 breakout rooms of 400, 200 and 120.

The congress center

Lyon’s congress center is located in between the Parc de la Tête d’Or and the Rhône at the North of the city. It is part of La Cité Internationale, an residential and event complex where all the amenities are built-in: from hotels, to restaurants, cinemas up to a museum. It benefits from direct connection by public transport from the city center.

The congress center offers 3 amphitheaters for 3,000 ; 900 & 300 people, and lot of modular space that can be set up in various layouts.
