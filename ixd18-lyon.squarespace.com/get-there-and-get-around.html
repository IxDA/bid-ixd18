Get there & get around

Lyon benefits from an exceptional geographic location: at the core of Europe, it is a transportation hub at the center of a dense network.

 

You can reach Lyon in one single shot from London via the Eurostar, and fly from Germany, Italy, Hungary, Greece, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Austria, Belgium and many more. Direct flights from Dubai, Montreal or Dakar will make the day of anyone a bit more remote.

Lyon is one of the biggest cities after Paris and is also gifted with an exceptional rail connection, one of our biggest French pride (our dear TGV hi speed train reaches 320km/h on regular service, making Lyon a little less than 2 hours from Paris). A great number of public transport means cross through the inner city from the outer ring of Lyon, offering nice and quick ways of getting from the airport to the very city center. The OnlyLyon committee offers great deals and discount tickets for our future attendants. Cherry on the cake: Lyon offers 30mn free rides on a tentacular network of easy access rental bikes! And anyway you’d only need 26mn to ride from Croix-Rousse up North to the Musée des Confluences in the very South of the city center.

 

Coming to Lyon

By plane 

Lyon Saint-Exupéry is the main airport. It is connected to the city via its dedicated tram line, Rhône Express. Lyon is also connected directly to Paris Charles de Gaule and Geneva Airport via a 2 hours direct train ride.

By train

Lyon is connected to all the major french and european cities. You can come directly from London in one single shot via the Eurostar. Both Lyon main stations (Part-Dieu and Perrache) are well connected to the metro network

Carpooling

Download the “Bla bla car” app and find a convenient carpooling trip to Lyon.

Moving in the City

Metro, tram & buses: Lyon has 5 efficient metro lines, 5 tram lines and 138 bus lines. The average ticket price is 1,80 €, you can get 10 in a bundle for a little more than 16 €. The weekly pass is around 15 €.

Rental bikes: The Vélo’v rental bike system is easy access and pretty cheap. Pick any bike for 1 € per day, then you can ride 30 minutes for free every time you pick a bike. More than 300 stations are available today and you can pick and leave your bike wherever you want. 

Uber and taxis: Uber is operating in Lyon, both with UberX and Uber Berline. An extensive network of taxis can get you anywhere in Lyon under 20€.

And of course…

  • Walking: many spots are at a walking distance when you wander in the city center.
  • Boats: depending on the season, a taxi-boat service connects the Presqu’île to the confluence area.
  • Segway? It seems that from time to time we see group of tourists proudly riding those elegant machines… #TrueFacts
